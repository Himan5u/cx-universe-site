Lie Detection through Microexpressions

Welcome to CX Universe’s Skill Learning Hub!

This section will teach you how to detect lies using microexpressions — quick facial expressions that reveal a person’s true emotions.

What are Microexpressions?

Microexpressions are involuntary facial expressions that occur within a fraction of a second. These expressions reveal a person’s genuine emotions, even when they are trying to hide them.

Why Learn Lie Detection?

Whether you’re dealing with clients, interviews, friends, or strangers — understanding microexpressions helps you detect dishonesty, nervousness, and hidden intentions.

Basic Emotions to Identify:

  • Happiness
  • Sadness
  • Fear
  • Disgust
  • Anger
  • Surprise
  • Contempt

How to Practice Lie Detection:

1. Watch interviews, reality shows, or political speeches in slow motion.

2. Pause and analyze facial expressions when someone is under pressure.

3. Focus on the eyes, eyebrows, and corners of the mouth — these areas show the most expressive micro-movements.

4. Take notes of facial changes and match them with emotions.

Important Tip:

Always compare what is being said with the person’s facial expressions. If the emotion doesn’t match the words — there’s a high chance they’re lying.

Keep Practicing!

Lie detection is a skill that grows with practice. Observe people, record notes, and test yourself often. Over time, you’ll start catching microexpressions automatically!

Remember:

Everyone lies. You just need to know where to look.
